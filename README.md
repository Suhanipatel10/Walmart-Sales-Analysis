📊 **Walmart Store Sales Prediction – EDA Dashboard**

This project focuses on post-launch product performance analysis using Walmart’s historical store sales dataset. The goal was to simulate evaluation of product health, user behavior, and business impact after launch.

🎯 **Objective**

After a certain time period:

* Are we driving consistent growth?
* What external factors influence performance?
* Where are the inefficiencies or risks?

This dashboard is built to translate raw sales data into actionable product insights, enabling better decision-making.

📈 **Key Metrics Tracked**
* Total Sales (6.7B+) → Overall business impact
* Average Weekly Sales → Short-term performance stability
* Monthly Trends → Seasonality & growth patterns
* Store Count → Distribution scale
* Fuel Price Impact → External dependency signal

These act as North Star + supporting metrics, similar to how PMs track KPIs post-launch.

🔍 **Insights & Analysis**
**1. Time-based Performance Trends**
Weekly fluctuations highlight seasonality and demand spikes
Identifies high-performing quarters → useful for campaign planning

**2. Store-level Variability**
Significant variation across stores
Indicates opportunities for:
localization strategies
inventory optimization

**3. Holiday Impact**
Majority of sales occur during non-holiday periods, but
Holiday weeks show sharp spikes → high-leverage windows

**4. Fuel Price vs Sales**
Weak correlation observed
Suggests low sensitivity to fuel fluctuations

**5. Temperature & External Factors**
Sales distribution varies across temperature ranges
Indicates potential behavioral or regional demand patterns

**6. Unemployment Trends**
Helps understand macroeconomic influence
Useful for forecasting demand risks

This project mimics a post-launch analytics workflow:

* Define KPIs (Sales, Growth, Stability)
* Segment Data (Store, Time, External Factors)
* Identify Patterns (Seasonality, Outliers)

🚀 **Key Product Insights**
* Sales are highly seasonal → timing matters more than volume
* Performance varies significantly across stores → one-size strategy fails
* External factors (fuel, unemployment) have limited direct impact
* Peak periods offer high ROI opportunities for intervention

This project demonstrates:

* Data-driven decision making
* KPI definition and tracking
* Translating analytics into business insights
* Thinking beyond charts → focusing on impact
